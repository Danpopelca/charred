About Us

How it started...

In the summer of 1967, a young chef named George Messick opened the doors to a small neighborhood grill with nothing more than an old charcoal pit, a few cast-iron pans, and an obsession for fire-kissed flavor. He had no investors, no marketing team, and no secret recipe — just calloused hands, a sharp blade, and a stubborn belief that food cooked over open flame tastes better.

The original Charred 1967 was no more than six tables tucked behind a butcher shop. Smoke would rise from the back alley grill each evening, drawing curious neighbors with the scent of sizzling steak and spiced wood. George didn’t talk much — he let the food speak. Every night, he trimmed his cuts by hand, seasoned them with instinct, and cooked them by feel, never time. Guests would sip lemonade or bring their own wine, sharing stories while George plated with quiet focus and firelight on his apron.

Word spread. One table turned to ten. Locals began calling it “the fire place” — not for warmth, but for the way it lit up the block with flavor. Charred 1967 wasn’t about fancy ingredients or Michelin stars. It was about smoke, simplicity, and soul. George believed that if he treated every steak like it was for his own family, people would come back. They did.

For over five decades, that same spirit has stayed lit. Passed down to the next generation, refined through new techniques, but still grounded in the same purpose: honest food, bold flame, and true hospitality. Today, Charred 1967 stands as a tribute to that original fire — the one that never needed a gas line to keep burning.

Today at Charred 1967

What began as a humble neighborhood grill has grown into a modern fine-dining experience — without losing its soul. Today, Charred 1967 is run by the same family, now led by Executive Chef Carl Messick, who continues to honor his father's fire-born philosophy with every plate served.

Our focus is simple: local, fresh, uncompromising ingredients. Every piece of produce is sourced from nearby farms. Every cut of meat is hand-selected and butchered in-house. We work closely with New Jersey and Pennsylvania purveyors to bring you seafood caught that same morning, herbs picked that same hour, and wines from vineyards we know by name.

Our kitchen burns with the same respect for fire as it did in 1967 — no shortcuts, no gas lines, just natural charcoal and a relentless commitment to flavor. From dry-aged ribeyes to seasonal vegetables roasted to perfection, we craft each dish with care, precision, and pride.

Charred 1967 isn't just a restaurant. It's a legacy of flame, flavor, and family — and we're proud to share that legacy with every guest who walks through our doors.
